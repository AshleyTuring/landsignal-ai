## Risk Factors

### Acquisition Risks:
- Properties at target price point (¬£100k-¬£300k) in London are rare
- Motivated sellers may have hidden issues (structural, contamination)
- Corporate portfolios may have strategic value (not actually for sale)

### Planning Risks:
- Local resistance to demolition (character, heritage concerns)
- Trend toward retrofit/refurb over demolition (climate policies)
- Longer decision times (12-18 months typical)
- Section 106 obligations eating into profit
- CIL (Community Infrastructure Levy) costs

### Build Cost Risks:
- Inflation in construction costs (2024-2025 volatile)
- ¬£2,000-¬£2,500/sqm may be optimistic for quality new build
- Utility connections (can add ¬£20k-¬£50k if not on site)
- Abnormal costs (contamination, piling, drainage)

### Market Risks:
- GDV assumptions based on current market (could fall)
- Outer London boroughs may have weaker demand
- Oversupply of new build in some areas
- Longer sales period (9-12 months) ties up capital

---

## Mitigation Strategies

### De-Risk Planning:
- Focus on LPAs with positive demolition/rebuild precedents
- Target plots with previous approvals (even if expired)
- Pre-application advice before acquisition (¬£2k-¬£5k)
- Site in areas with housing need/allocations

### De-Risk Build Costs:
- Get QS estimates on target properties before committing
- Build in 20% contingency
- Use proven contractors with fixed-price contracts
- Modular/modern methods of construction for cost certainty

### De-Risk Acquisition:
- Full structural survey before exchange
- Environmental desktop study (contamination check)
- Title checks with solicitor (access, covenants, restrictions)
- Negotiate subject-to-planning clauses (or option agreements)
