## Approach A: Precedent Replication - 8-Storey Mixed-Use (Slough Focus)

- Option agreement: £20,000-£50,000
- Planning consultancy: £30,000-£50,000
- Legals: £10,000-£20,000
- **Total:** £60,000-£120,000 (exit at planning stage)

**Expected Results:** 10-30 properties (Slough + similar towns)

**SearchLand AI Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready):**
```
1. Proposal: contains "mixed-use" AND "storey"
2. Proposal: contains "retail" OR "commercial"
3. Use class: contains any [E - Commercial Business and Service, C3 - Dwellinghouses]
4. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.15 AND less than 1.5
5. Max building height on title (m): is less than 15
6. Distance to nearest Train Station (m): is less than 1000
7. Latest sold date: is before 2015-01-01
8. Applications in the last 5 years: is less than 3
9. Planning constraints: does not contain [Conservation Area, Listed Building(s) in plot, Flood Zone 3]
10. Building on title is inactive: Yes
```

**Rationale:**
The approved 8-storey mixed-use in Slough provides **proven planning precedent**. This approach replicates that success by targeting:
- Similar use class combinations (E retail + C3 residential)
- Sites currently low-rise (<15m = ~4 storeys) with intensification potential
- Transport accessibility (<1km station) supporting density policy
- Long-hold properties (sold before 2015) indicating unmotivated/passive ownership
- Inactive buildings suggesting stalled development or tired landlord

**Profit Path:** 
- Secure option £30k → Apply for 8-storey mixed-use consent (£50k) → Sell to developer with planning at £800k-£1.2M → **Net profit £700k-£1.1M per deal**
- Target 2-3 deals to reach £2M profit target

**Strengths:**
- Precedent reduces planning risk (local authority already approved similar scheme)
- Low capital via option agreements (£30k-£50k vs £500k+ outright purchase)
- Transport proximity supports planning policy for intensification
- Inactive buildings = motivation signal (no income, potential sell pressure)
- Slough regeneration focus aligns with mixed-use policy support

**Limitations:**
- Precedent may not transfer perfectly to different sites (microclimate planning)
- Low planning activity (<3 apps) may indicate planning challenges not visible in data
- Requires negotiation skills for option agreements
- Planning process 12-18 months (longer timeline)
- Local opposition risk (NIMBYism for height)

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. **Weeks 1-4:** Run SearchLand filter, export results, conduct desktop due diligence (Land Registry, planning portal)
2. **Weeks 5-8:** Shortlist 10 sites with best precedent match, approach owners with option proposal
3. **Weeks 9-12:** Negotiate option agreements (12-24 month term, £30k-£50k fee, planning-contingent)
4. **Months 4-6:** Engage planning consultancy, prepare pre-app consultation with LPA
5. **Months 7-18:** Submit planning application citing Slough precedent, manage process
6. **Months 19-20:** Upon approval, market to housebuilders/developers, complete sale
7. **Exit:** Assign option or complete purchase + immediate sale at planning premium

## Approach B: Liquidation Arbitrage - Forced Sales at Discount

**Strategic Pattern:** Pattern 2D (Liquidation/Administration Sales)  
**Capital Required:**
- Acquisition (distressed pricing): £120,000-£180,000
- Legals/surveys: £10,000-£20,000
- **Total:** £130,000-£200,000 (full budget utilization)

**Expected Results:** 5-15 properties (UK-wide, highly targeted)

**SearchLand AI Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready):**
```
1. Ownership type: is equal to Company
2. Company status: is one of [Liquidation, In Administration]
3. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.2 AND less than 2
4. Use class: contains any [E - Commercial Business and Service, C3 - Dwellinghouses, B2 - General industrial, B8 - Storage or distribution]
5. Distance to nearest Train Station (m): is less than 1500
6. Planning constraints: does not contain [Flood Zone 3, SSSI, Ancient Woodland]
7. Building on title is derelict: Yes
8. Percentage of plot un-constrained: is greater than 70
```

**Rationale:**
This approach exploits **maximum behavioral motivation** - companies in liquidation/administration are **legally obligated to sell assets** to repay creditors. Key advantages:
- Liquidators prioritize speed over price (time = cost)
- No emotional attachment (corporate sellers vs family homes)
- Clear decision-maker (liquidator has authority, no committee delays)
- Often 20-40% below market value due to distressed circumstances
- Derelict buildings = double motivation (no income + liabilities)

**Profit Path:**
- Acquire at £150k (30% discount to £215k market value) → Secure planning consent or sell to developer as-is → Exit at £400k-£600k with planning or £250k-£300k as-is → **Profit £100k-£450k per deal**
- Complete 4-5 deals to reach £2M target

**Strengths:**
- **Strongest motivation signal** (forced sale = maximum leverage)
- Fast decision-making (liquidators can't wait)
- Below-market pricing typical (20-40% discount)
- Clear legal process (no gazumping, no chain)
- Cash buyers highly preferred (speed advantage)
- Derelict buildings = value-add opportunity (planning upside)

**Limitations:**
- **Very limited pool** (liquidations rare, <10 results per LPA typical)
- Properties often have issues (structural, contamination, legal complications)
- Other creditors may have claims (secured vs unsecured debt hierarchy)
- Due diligence time-pressured (liquidators want quick completion)
- May require significant refurb/remediation (reduce profit margin)

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. **Week 1:** Run filter, identify 5-15 liquidation/administration cases
2. **Weeks 2-3:** Research company status (Companies House), contact liquidator/administrator directly
3. **Weeks 4-5:** Desktop due diligence (title search, planning history, contamination risk assessment)
4. **Week 6:** Submit offers (20-30% below market, emphasize cash + speed)
5. **Weeks 7-10:** Negotiate with liquidator, conduct surveys (structural, environmental)
6. **Weeks 11-14:** Legal completion (fast-track conveyancing)
7. **Months 4-12:** Apply for planning consent OR sell immediately to developer as-is
8. **Exit Strategy A:** Sell with planning at £400k-£600k (£250k-£450k profit)
9. **Exit Strategy B:** Flip as-is to developer at £250k-£300k (£100k-£150k profit, faster)

## Approach C: Airspace Rights Acquisition - Low-Rise to 8-Storey

**Strategic Pattern:** Pattern 6A (Airspace Development)  
**Capital Required:**
- Airspace rights purchase: £80,000-£120,000
- Planning costs: £40,000-£60,000
- Legals/surveys: £10,000-£20,000
- **Total:** £130,000-£200,000

**Expected Results:** 15-40 properties (urban areas, London commuter belt)

**SearchLand AI Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready):**
```
1. A building in the title has flat roof: Yes
2. Max building height on title (m): is less than 12
3. Use class: contains any [E - Commercial Business and Service, C3 - Dwellinghouses]
4. PTAL: contains any [4, 5, 6a, 6b (Best)]
5. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.1 AND less than 0.8
6. Number of buildings on title: is equal to 1
7. Tenure: is one of [Freehold]
8. Planning constraints: does not contain [Conservation Area, Listed Building(s) in plot]
9. Percentage of plot un-constrained: is greater than 80
10. Distance to nearest Train Station (m): is less than 800
```

**Rationale:**
This approach targets **structural arbitrage** - purchasing airspace rights above existing low-rise buildings rather than full property ownership. Key insight:
- Flat roofs = easier structural addition (no hip/gable complications)
- <12m height = ~3-4 storeys, significant headroom for 8-storey target
- High PTAL (4-6) = policy support for density intensification
- Freehold = single negotiation (no head lease complications)
- Single building = simpler structural assessment

**Profit Path:**
- Airspace rights £100k → Planning consent £50k → Sell airspace with consent to developer/investor → Exit at £600k-£1M → **Profit £450k-£850k per deal**
- Target 2-3 deals to reach £2M target

**Strengths:**
- **Very low capital** (airspace rights 20-40% of freehold value)
- Landowner keeps ground floor income (easier negotiation)
- High PTAL = strong planning policy support for density
- Flat roof = lower structural costs (no roof reconstruction)
- Growing strategy (housing crisis = policy push for airspace)
- Fast planning (if policy-compliant, 6-12 months)

**Limitations:**
- **Structural capacity unknown** until survey (£10k-£15k cost, risk if unsuitable)
- Party wall agreements required (neighbor complications)
- Freeholder negotiation essential (no leasehold airspace without consent)
- Fire safety regulations stringent (post-Grenfell, expensive compliance)
- Construction noise/disruption (existing occupiers may object)
- Requires specialist structural engineer and architect (£30k-£50k fees)

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. **Weeks 1-3:** Run filter (focus on London, Reading, Slough, Brighton - high PTAL areas)
2. **Weeks 4-6:** Desktop screening (OS maps for roof type, Google Streetview for condition)
3. **Weeks 7-10:** Approach freeholders with airspace proposal (profit-share or fixed fee)
4. **Weeks 11-14:** Negotiate airspace option/purchase (£80k-£120k)
5. **Months 4-6:** Structural survey (load-bearing capacity), engage architect for scheme design
6. **Months 7-12:** Submit planning application (4-5 additional storeys citing PTAL/density policy)
7. **Months 13-15:** Upon planning approval, market airspace with consent to developers
8. **Exit:** Sell airspace rights + planning consent for £600k-£1M (no construction required)

**Alternative Exit:** JV with developer - they fund construction, you receive profit share at completion (higher profit, longer timeline)

- Elderly, non-professional landlords

**SearchLand AI Prompt (Copy-Paste Ready):**
```
1. Currently has HMO: Yes
2. HMO License Expiry Date: is before 2025-01-01

4. EPC rating: contains any [E, F, G]
5. Latest sold date: is before 2010-01-01
6. Use class: contains any [C3 - Dwellinghouses, C4 - HMO]

8. Planning constraints: does not contain [Article 4 Directions]
9. Distance to nearest Train Station (m): is less than 1500

```

**Rationale:**
This approach exploits **regulatory deadline pressure** combined with **tired landlord signals**:
- **Expired HMO license** = illegal to operate (cannot rent until renewed, £1k-£3k renewal + compliance costs)
- **Low EPC (E-G)** = double regulatory pressure (cannot rent below E since 2020, improvement £5k-£50k)
- **Long hold (sold before 2010)** = 15+ years ownership, tired landlord likely
- **NOT Article 4** = can renew license if owner wants (avoids dead-end situations)

Owner's dilemma: Spend £10k-£50k (renewal + EPC improvement + compliance) OR sell and exit

**Profit Path:**
- Acquire at £170k (20-30% discount to £215k-£245k market) → Renew HMO license £2k + EPC improvement £15k-£25k → Rent as HMO (5-8% yield) OR sell as compliant HMO to investor at £280k-£320k → **Profit £68k-£113k per deal**

**Strengths:**
- **Forced action** (expired license = cannot operate legally)
- **Time pressure** (local authority enforcement risk)
- Tired landlord (15+ years ownership = may want exit)
- Low EPC = double motivation (two regulatory problems)
- Cash buyer advantage (speed = premium in distressed scenarios)
- Can operate as HMO post-purchase (income during hold period)
- Multiple exit strategies (hold for yield, flip to investor, convert to C3)

**Limitations:**
- **Limited pool** (expired licenses rare, LAs enforce renewals)
- Renewal costs reduce margin (£2k license + £15k-£25k EPC)
- Article 4 areas excluded (can't renew even if we want)
- Some HMOs may have structural/fire safety issues (survey critical)
- Local authority may not renew license (enforcement history, saturation)
- Volume strategy required (£68k-£113k profit per deal = need many deals)

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. **Weeks 1-2:** Run filter, prioritize areas with mandatory HMO licensing (London boroughs, university towns)
2. **Weeks 3-4:** Contact owners directly (expired license = not marketed, off-market opportunity)
3. **Weeks 5-6:** Emphasize owner's pain: "Renewal costs £10k-£50k, enforcement risk, let us solve your problem"
4. **Weeks 7-10:** Negotiate purchase at 20-30% discount (owner avoids renewal costs + time)
5. **Weeks 11-14:** Legal completion (fast-track, cash buyer advantage)
6. **Months 4-6:** Renew HMO license (£2k), complete EPC improvements (£15k-£25k)
7. **Exit Strategy A:** Hold for rental yield (5-8% gross yield, £12k-£18k annual income)
8. **Exit Strategy B:** Sell to HMO investor at £280k-£320k (£68k-£113k profit, 6-month turnaround)
9. **Exit Strategy C:** Convert to C3 single dwelling if market supports (removes HMO stigma)

**Volume Approach:** Need 18-30 deals @ £68k-£113k each to reach £2M target (portfolio strategy over 3-4 years)
