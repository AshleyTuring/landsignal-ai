# Claude's Independent Approaches

## Approach A: Precedent Replication - Mixed-Use High-Density Zones

**Strategic Pattern:** Pattern #14 (Planning Precedent Analysis) + Pattern #4 (Low Capital Entry)  
**Capital Required:** £150,000-£190,000  
**Expected Results:** 15-30 properties UK-wide

**SearchLand AI Prompt:**
```
1. Proposal: contains "mixed-use"
2. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.198 AND less than 2.47
3. Max building height on title (m): is greater than 15
4. Distance to nearest Train Station: is less than 800
5. Use class (current or previous): contains any "A1" "A2" "E" "Sui Generis"
6. Ownership type: is "Company"
7. Less than 1 application in last 5 years: is "No"
8. Last application submitted: is after "2020-01-01"
9. Sales listing price: is less than 200000
```

**Rationale:**
The reference planning application shows success factors: mixed-use, high density (8 storeys), good transport (PTAL 3-4), Class E+C3. This approach finds properties where similar developments have already been proven viable through recent planning activity. The "less than 1 application in last 5 years = No" means there HAS been planning activity (avoiding stalled sites), and corporate ownership provides negotiation leverage for option agreements.

**Strengths:**
- De-risked by recent planning precedent (applications post-2020)
- Height filter (>15m) ensures buildings can support additional storeys
- Transport accessibility maximizes GDV for residential component
- Corporate ownership enables creative deal structures (options, joint ventures)
- Budget allows for multiple site options within £200k

**Limitations:**
- Competition from other developers tracking similar sites
- May require complex negotiations with corporate owners
- Recent planning activity could mean higher asking prices
- Transport accessibility might inflate land values in some areas

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. Run search UK-wide, then filter by PTAL 3-4 areas manually
2. Research each property's planning history for approval patterns
3. Approach owners with option agreement proposals (£30k-£50k per site)
4. Structure deals: Small upfront payment + profit share on planning consent
5. Target 3-4 properties within budget to spread risk

**Capital Breakdown:**
- Option agreements: £120,000-£160,000 (3-4 sites @ £30k-£50k each)
- Planning consultant: £20,000
- Legal fees: £10,000-£15,000
- Due diligence: £5,000
- **Total:** £155,000-£190,000

## Approach B: Corporate Distress + Lease Urgency (Time Arbitrage)

**Strategic Pattern:** Pattern #13 (Time/Information Arbitrage) + Pattern #3 (Motivation Signals)  
**Capital Required:** £100,000-£150,000  
**Expected Results:** 5-15 properties (highly motivated)

**SearchLand AI Prompt:**
```
1. Ownership type: is "Company"
2. Company status: is "Liquidation" OR is "Administration"
3. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.198 AND less than 5
4. Max building height on title (m): is greater than 12
5. Building on title: is "Active" OR is "Inactive"
6. Use class (current or previous): contains any "A1" "A2" "A3" "E" "B1" "Sui Generis"
7. Distance to nearest Train Station: is less than 1000
8. Tenure: is "Freehold" OR is "Leasehold"
```

**Rationale:**
Combines HIGHEST motivation signals (liquidation/administration) with timing urgency. Companies in distress need quick asset disposals. The 24-hour SearchLand refresh cycle gives us early access to newly listed distressed properties. By targeting larger plots (up to 5 acres) with existing buildings 12m+ height, we can propose airspace/redevelopment schemes that match our 8-storey precedent. This is pure time arbitrage - first contact wins.

**Strengths:**
- EXTREME seller motivation (legal obligation to liquidate assets)
- Time arbitrage advantage (24-hour data refresh)
- Larger plot range allows for creative schemes
- Existing building height suggests planning will consider taller proposals
- Freehold or leasehold flexibility
- Budget enables fast deposits to secure deals

**Limitations:**
- Small pool of distressed assets
- Administrator may demand market value despite urgency
- Properties might have complex title issues or charges
- Building condition could add unexpected costs
- Race against other opportunistic buyers

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. Run search daily (exploit 24-hour refresh cycle)
2. Contact administrators IMMEDIATELY upon listing (within hours)
3. Offer fast completion (7-14 days) as negotiation leverage
4. Structure as option agreement or conditional purchase
5. Focus on sites with existing planning consent or allocation
6. Have legal/planning consultants on standby

**Capital Breakdown:**
- Land acquisition/options: £60,000-£100,000 (below market due to distress)
- Fast-track legal: £15,000-£20,000
- Planning consultant: £15,000-£20,000
- Emergency surveys: £10,000
- **Total:** £100,000-£150,000

## Approach C: Airspace Development Rights (Low Capital, High ROI)

**Strategic Pattern:** Pattern #4 (Low Capital Entry) + Pattern #19 (Height/Volume Analysis)  
**Capital Required:** £120,000-£180,000  
**Expected Results:** 10-25 properties (flat-roof commercial)

**SearchLand AI Prompt:**
```
1. Max building height on title (m): is greater than 12 AND less than 20
2. Building on title: is "Active"
3. Use class (current or previous): contains any "E" "A1" "A2" "B1"
4. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.05 AND less than 1
5. Tenure: is "Freehold"
6. Ownership type: is "Private owner" OR is "Company"
7. Distance to nearest Train Station: is less than 600
8. Less than 1 application in last 5 years: is "Yes"
9. EPC rating: is "D" OR is "E" OR is "F"
```

**Rationale:**
Targets 4-6 storey commercial buildings (12-20m) with flat roofs in prime locations where we can add 2-4 residential floors to reach our 8-storey precedent. Owners have never explored airspace value ("less than 1 application = Yes" means NO planning attempts). Low EPC ratings suggest under-invested buildings where owners might welcome capital injection. Freehold gives us negotiating leverage for airspace rights purchase/lease. This avoids full property acquisition - we only buy the AIR above.

**Strengths:**
- Lowest capital requirement (buying air rights, not land)
- Owners often unaware of airspace value
- Active buildings mean income continues during planning
- No planning history = owners haven't considered development
- Multiple properties feasible within budget (10-15 airspace deals @ £10k-£20k each)
- Transport proximity maximizes residential GDV
- Low EPC = motivated owners (facing compliance costs)

**Limitations:**
- Requires educating owners on airspace concept
- Complex legal agreements for airspace rights
- Structural assessments needed (can building support additional floors?)
- Party wall agreements required
- Existing tenants might complicate access

**Implementation Strategy:**
1. Identify 4-6 storey flat-roof commercial buildings
2. Commission structural pre-assessments (desktop studies)
3. Approach owners with "free" planning gain proposition
4. Structure as: £10k-£20k upfront + 30-40% profit share OR outright purchase of airspace rights for £80k-£120k
5. Secure airspace rights on 10-15 properties
6. Submit simultaneous planning applications for residential additions
7. Sell planning-approved sites or JV with builder

**Capital Breakdown:**
- Airspace rights (10-15 properties): £100,000-£150,000 (@ £10k-£15k each OR 3-4 outright @ £30k-£40k)
- Structural surveys: £15,000-£20,000
- Planning consultant: £15,000-£25,000
- Legal fees (airspace agreements): £20,000-£30,000
- **Total:** £150,000-£225,000 (ADJUST to target range: aim for lower end)
- **Optimized Total:** £120,000-£180,000 (focus on profit-share deals to reduce upfront)

1. **Approach A (Precedent):** Safety-first replication of proven success
