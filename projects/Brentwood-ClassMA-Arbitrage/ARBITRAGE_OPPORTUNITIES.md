### CATEGORY 1: Emerging Allocation Arbitrage (Policy Adoption)

**Mechanism:** Buy land in "emerging" local plan allocations BEFORE the plan is adopted. When plan is adopted ‚Üí land value increases 2-5x.

**SearchLand Preset:** "Emerging sites without planning"
- **Description:** Freehold land (4k-40k sqm) in an "emerging" housing allocation with no planning yet
- **Strategy:** Secure the land, wait for the policy to be adopted, get consent, and flip
- **Baseline (17 LPAs):** ~53 sites

**Why This Works:**
1. Councils publish draft local plans (emerging allocations)
2. Sites get allocated for housing/development
3. Plan goes through consultation (12-24 months)
4. Plan adopted ‚Üí allocation becomes policy ‚Üí value uplift
5. You buy during "emerging" phase at lower price

**Profit Mechanism:**
- Emerging allocation land: ¬£50k-¬£200k/acre
- Adopted allocation land: ¬£200k-¬£500k/acre
- Your profit: 2-5x if you wait for adoption

**Risk:** Plan may not be adopted, allocation may be removed, timelines unpredictable

### CATEGORY 2: Lease Expiry Arbitrage (Predictable Events)

**Mechanism:** Contact commercial landlords 3-6 months BEFORE lease expires. Offer to buy before they face void period.

#### Preset A: "Commercial with expiring leases"
- **Description:** Medium commercial sites with leases ending before 2027
- **Baseline (17 LPAs):** 153 sites
- **Strategy:** Buy at discount (landlord avoids void), convert to residential (Class MA)

#### Preset B: "Fast food restaurant sites (expired leases)"
- **Description:** Fast food or caf√© sites with expired leases and undeveloped land
- **Baseline (17 LPAs):** 56 sites
- **Strategy:** Tenant already left, landlord has vacant unit, maximum motivation

**Why This Works:**
1. Lease expiry date is PUBLIC information (Land Registry)
2. Landlord faces: void period, rates liability, finding new tenant
3. You offer certainty: cash purchase, quick completion
4. Discount: 10-20% below market (certainty premium)

**Profit Mechanism:**
- Commercial value: ¬£300k
- Your purchase (motivated): ¬£240k-¬£270k (10-20% discount)
- Convert to residential (Class MA): +¬£150k build cost
- Sell as flats: ¬£600k-¬£800k
- Your profit: ¬£130k-¬£260k

### CATEGORY 3: Licensing Compliance Arbitrage (Regulatory Pressure)

**Mechanism:** Find landlords with expired/expiring licenses who face compliance pressure.

#### Preset: "HMO expired licences"
- **Description:** Houses in multiple occupation with expired licences
- **Baseline (17 LPAs):** 331 sites

- Your profit: ¬£40k-¬£80k immediate equity

### CATEGORY 4: Policy Change Arbitrage (Regulatory Shifts)

**Mechanism:** Monitor for regulatory changes that instantly create value uplift.

#### Example A: Article 4 Direction Lifted
- **What:** Council removes Article 4 direction from an area
- **Effect:** Permitted Development rights (Class MA, etc.) suddenly apply
- **Arbitrage:** Properties in that area can now convert commercial‚Üíresidential without planning
- **Challenge:** Can't filter for "Article 4 lifted" in SearchLand - requires council monitoring

#### Example B: Use Class Changes
- **What:** Government creates new use classes (e.g., Class E in 2020)
- **Effect:** Existing properties gain conversion rights they didn't have before
- **Arbitrage:** Buy Class E property ‚Üí convert to residential via Class MA
- **SearchLand Filter:** `Use class: contains any [E - Commercial Business and Service]`

#### Example C: Green Belt Review
- **What:** Council releases sites from Green Belt in local plan review
- **Effect:** Land value increases 5-20x (Green Belt ‚Üí developable)
- **Arbitrage:** Identify sites likely to be released (council consultation documents)
- **SearchLand Proxy:** Monitor Green Belt sites near existing settlements

#### Example D: EPC Minimum Standard Changes (April 2025)
- **What:** New EPC regulations make F/G rated properties unlettable
- **Effect:** Landlords with F/G properties face: upgrade OR sell
- **Arbitrage:** Buy at discount from landlords who can't afford upgrade
- **SearchLand Filter:** `EPC rating: is one of [F, G]`

### CATEGORY 5: Simple Form Filing Arbitrage

**Mechanism:** Buy property, file a simple form/application, value increases.

#### Example A: Certificate of Lawfulness (CLEUD/CLOPUD)
- **What:** Confirm existing use or proposed use is lawful
- **Cost:** ¬£200-¬£500 application fee
- **Effect:** Provides certainty to buyers, increases marketability
- **Arbitrage:** Buy property with unclear planning status, file CLEUD, sell with certainty

#### Example B: Prior Approval (Class MA, Class Q, etc.)
- **What:** Simplified application for permitted development
- **Cost:** ¬£100-¬£200 application fee
- **Timeline:** 56 days determination
- **Effect:** Confirms conversion rights, value increases
- **Arbitrage:** Buy commercial property, file Prior Approval, sell with consent

#### Example C: Discharge of Conditions
- **What:** Property has planning permission with unmet conditions
- **Effect:** Until conditions discharged, consent can't be implemented
- **Arbitrage:** Buy property with unimplemented consent, discharge conditions, value increases
- **Your reference case (Hillingdon):** This is exactly what that application was for
