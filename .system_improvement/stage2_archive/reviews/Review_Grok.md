## PART 2: NEW PATTERNS PROPOSED

Based on reviewing the raw presets in PresetFiltersBuiltIntoSearchLand.md, I identified several patterns Claude missed or could have elevated further. Here are 4 new patterns:

## NEW PATTERN: Agricultural Land Conversion

**Category:** Rural Development Strategies

**Strategic Rationale:**
Agricultural land represents vast opportunity for development, but requires careful navigation of planning policies. Lower-grade agricultural land (Grade 3b/4/5) has weaker protection than prime agricultural land, making conversion more feasible. This pattern targets sites where farming economics make landowners receptive to diversification.

**Key Filters:**
1. Agricultural Land Grade: is one of 3b, 4, 5 (NOT 1-2)
2. Use class: is equal to Agricultural
3. Title area (acres): is greater than 5
4. Distance to nearest built up area (m): is less than 1000
5. Ownership type: is equal to Private owner

**Sourced From:**
- Preset #39 (Nutrient Neutrality) - but broader application
- General agricultural economics knowledge
- Observed gap in rural development strategies

**Use Cases:**
- Rural housing schemes on lower-grade land
- Agricultural diversification (e.g., polytunnels to housing)
- Sites where farming income insufficient for viability

**Expected Results:**
- 50-200 results per rural LPA
- Conversion rates: 10-20% with proper approach
- Capital: £100k-£500k per acre for development

## NEW PATTERN: Listed Building Opportunities

**Category:** Heritage Development Strategies

**Strategic Rationale:**
Listed buildings offer development potential through sympathetic extensions, conversions, or change of use, but require heritage expertise. Many listed buildings are underutilized or in poor condition, creating motivated owners. This pattern targets sites where listing status can be leveraged rather than avoided.

**Key Filters:**
1. Listed building(s) in plot: Yes
2. Building on title is derelict: is equal to Yes
3. Use class: is one of Residential institutions, Commercial
4. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.1
5. Distance to nearest conservation area (m): is less than 500

**Sourced From:**
- Preset #37 (Educational sites) - but broader heritage application
- General heritage development knowledge
- Observed gap in constraint-based strategies

**Use Cases:**
- Listed building conversions to residential
- Heritage asset enhancement schemes
- Sites requiring sympathetic development

**Expected Results:**
- 20-100 results per historic LPA
- Planning success: 60-80% with heritage consultant
- Capital: £200k-£1M+ depending on scale

## NEW PATTERN: Flood Risk Area Redevelopment

**Category:** Constraint Workaround Strategies

**Strategic Rationale:**
Flood risk areas are often undervalued despite development potential. Sequential test and exception policies allow development in Flood Zone 3 with proper mitigation. Many sites have existing development, reducing flood risk concerns. This pattern targets brownfield sites in flood areas where redevelopment is permitted.

**Key Filters:**
1. Percentage of title covered by Flood Zone 2: is less than 50
2. Percentage of title covered by Flood Zone 3: is greater than 10
3. Developed area percentage: is greater than 25
4. Use class: is one of Industrial, Commercial
5. Title area (acres): is greater than 0.5

**Sourced From:**
- Preset #52 (Bio-energy) - flood zone exclusions
- General flood risk policy knowledge
- Observed gap in environmental constraint strategies

**Use Cases:**
- Brownfield redevelopment in flood areas
- Flood-resilient housing schemes
- Sites where existing development proves viability

**Expected Results:**
- 30-150 results per flood-prone LPA
- Planning success: 40-60% with flood risk assessment
- Capital: £150k-£750k with mitigation costs

## NEW PATTERN: Conservation Area Enhancement

**Category:** Heritage Development Strategies

**Strategic Rationale:**
Conservation areas protect character but allow appropriate development. Many properties in conservation areas are tired or underutilized, creating opportunities for enhancement schemes. Local authorities often support improvements that enhance character. This pattern targets sites where conservation status can be used to access grants and permissions.

**Key Filters:**
1. Percentage of title covered by Conservation Areas: is greater than 50
2. Building on title is inactive: is equal to Yes
3. EPC rating: is one of E, F, G
4. Use class: is one of Residential, Commercial
5. Distance to nearest listed building (m): is less than 200

**Sourced From:**
- Preset #33 (Class ZA) - conservation area exclusions
- General conservation area policy knowledge
- Observed gap in area-based strategies

**Use Cases:**
- Conservation area regeneration projects
- Heritage grant-funded improvements
- Sites requiring character enhancement

**Expected Results:**

- Capital: £100k-£400k with potential grants

## PART 3: STRUCTURE & USABILITY

### Structural Improvement: Master Pattern Index

**Current Problem:**
The document has patterns scattered across categories without a clear navigation index. Users must scroll through to find relevant patterns.

**Proposed Solution:**
Add a comprehensive index at the top with pattern numbers, names, capital ranges, risk levels, and key use cases. Include quick-reference tags for Round mapping.

**Example:**
```
## Master Pattern Index

| # | Pattern Name | Capital | Risk | Best For | Round |
|---|--------------|---------|------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Expired Planning | £50k-£200k | Med | Planning expertise | 2 |
| 2 | Motivated Seller | £100k-£500k | Low-Med | Negotiation | 4 |
...
```

**Benefit:**
Allows quick pattern selection based on constraints, reducing time to find relevant strategies.

### Structural Improvement: Pattern Interconnections

**Current Problem:**
Patterns are presented in isolation, but many work best in combination (e.g., Motivated Seller + PDR).

**Proposed Solution:**
Add "Pattern Combinations" section with proven synergies and example applications.

**Example:**
```
## Pattern Combinations

**High-ROI Combo: Pattern 2A + 7A**
- Motivated corporate seller + Class MA PDR
- Capital: £100k-£300k
- Why: Fast execution, motivated seller accepts lower profit split
- Example: Derelict commercial building owned by company 10+ years
```

**Benefit:**
Helps users combine patterns for more effective strategies rather than using them singly.

### Structural Improvement: Decision Tree Navigation

**Current Problem:**
No guidance on which pattern to choose based on user constraints.

**Proposed Solution:**
Add interactive decision tree at the beginning.

**Example:**
```
## Quick Pattern Selector

**Capital Available?**
- <£50k → See Patterns 4A, 13A
- £50k-£200k → See Patterns 1, 4, 6, 7, 8, 9
- >£500k → See Patterns 3, 5, 10, 11

**Timeline?**
- <6 months → PDR patterns (7)
- 6-12 months → Regulation-driven (8), Motivated seller (2)
- 1-2 years → Planning-based (1, 3, 5)
```

**Benefit:**
Reduces decision paralysis by guiding users to appropriate patterns based on their situation.

**Current Problem:**

**Proposed Solution:**
Add "Reverse Lookup" table showing which patterns use each preset.

**Example:**
